Privacy Policy

Parachord Browser Extension — Last updated February 25, 2026

Overview

The Parachord browser extension connects supported music websites to the Parachord desktop application running on your computer. This policy explains what information the extension accesses, how it is used, and what protections are in place.

What the Extension Accesses

The extension accesses the following information only on supported music sites (YouTube, Bandcamp, Spotify, Apple Music, SoundCloud, Last.fm, ListenBrainz, and Pitchfork):

  • Page URLs — to detect when you are on a supported site and to send music links to the desktop app when you choose to.
  • Page content — content scripts read media playback state (play, pause, track title, artist, album, progress) so the desktop app can display and control what is playing.
  • Tab information — tab IDs are used to route playback commands to the correct browser tab.

How Information Is Used

All information accessed by the extension is sent exclusively to the Parachord desktop application running on your own computer via Chrome’s native messaging API. This is a direct, local-only communication channel managed by the browser — no data leaves your machine.

The extension uses this information to:

  • Show the currently playing track in the desktop app.
  • Forward playback commands (play, pause, skip) from the desktop app to the browser.
  • Send music URLs and playlist data you choose to share to the desktop app.
  • Display a badge icon indicating connection status and site support.

Local Storage

The extension stores a small amount of settings data locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local. This includes preferences such as whether Spotify or Apple Music link interception is enabled. This data never leaves your browser.

What the Extension Does NOT Do

  • Does not collect, transmit, or store personal information.
  • Does not send any data to remote servers, analytics services, or third parties.
  • Does not track your browsing history or activity outside of supported music sites.
  • Does not inject advertisements or modify page content for commercial purposes.
  • Does not sell, share, or monetize any data.

Permissions Explained

  • activeTab / tabs — needed to detect which tab is playing music and to send commands to it.
  • scripting — needed to inject content scripts that read playback state on supported sites.
  • nativeMessaging — needed to communicate with the Parachord desktop app via a secure, local-only channel.
  • contextMenus — adds a “Send to Parachord” option when you right-click on supported links or pages.
  • storage — stores your extension preferences locally.
  • alarms — keeps the background service worker alive to maintain the connection with the desktop app.
  • webNavigation — detects navigation to music URLs for optional link interception (Spotify, Apple Music).
  • Host permissions — content scripts run only on the specific music sites listed in the manifest (YouTube, Bandcamp, Spotify, Apple Music, SoundCloud, Last.fm, ListenBrainz, Pitchfork).

Third-Party Services

The extension itself does not communicate with any third-party service. The Parachord desktop application may connect to music services (Spotify, Apple Music, Last.fm, etc.) based on your configuration, but that is governed by the desktop application and each service’s own privacy policy — not this extension.

Data Security

Communication between the extension and the desktop app uses Chrome’s native messaging API, which ensures that only the registered Parachord desktop application can exchange messages with the extension. No network sockets or HTTP connections are used for this communication.

Children’s Privacy

The extension does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
